About Sector Chess

🎮 About the Game

Sector Chess is a full-featured browser chess game built for the modern era. Wrapped in the distinctive Sector Arcade neon aesthetic, it delivers a complete chess experience that works instantly in any browser — no download, no account, no cost.

Whether you're a complete beginner looking to learn the game or an experienced player seeking a challenge, Sector Chess adapts to your level. The AI offers three difficulty tiers: Beginner uses random moves to help you learn patterns, Advanced applies a depth-3 minimax algorithm with positional heuristics, and Master mode uses depth-4 minimax with alpha-beta pruning — a genuine test for experienced players.

Beyond single-player, Sector Chess features real-time online PvP through Firebase. Create a room, share the code with a friend anywhere in the world, and play instantly. A live chat system keeps communication open during games, and a complete move history log tracks every decision in algebraic notation.

Eight board themes — from Classic and Wood to Neon and Marble — and five piece styles let you customize the visual experience. The audio engine synthesizes all sounds in-browser: piece moves, captures, castling, check alerts, and checkmate all have distinct sound design.

🎯 How to Play

  • Click or tap a piece to select it — legal moves are highlighted on the board.
  • Click or tap the destination square to move. Invalid moves are rejected automatically.
  • Play against AI (3 difficulty levels) or create an online room to challenge a friend.
  • The move log on the right tracks all moves in standard algebraic notation.
  • Use the Undo button to take back moves in local games.
  • Pawn promotion triggers a selection modal — choose Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

💡 Chess Tips & Tricks

  • Control the center — placing pawns and knights on central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5) gives your pieces maximum mobility.
  • Develop your pieces early — move knights and bishops out before the middle game to get your army into position.
  • Castle early for king safety — never leave your king in the center once development begins.
  • Don't bring your queen out too early — it's valuable and easily chased by opponent pieces, wasting moves.
  • Think about your opponent's threats — before every move, ask yourself what they're planning and respond accordingly.
  • Rooks are strongest on open files — position them where no pawns block their path for maximum firepower.
  • Passed pawns are powerful in the endgame — a pawn with no opposing pawns blocking it can become a queen.
  • Practice against Beginner AI first — it plays randomly, letting you experiment with tactics without pressure.
