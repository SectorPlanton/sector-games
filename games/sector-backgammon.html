About Sector Backgammon

🎮 About the Game

Sector Backgammon brings one of history's oldest board games — played for over 5,000 years — into the modern neon-lit browser experience. It implements the complete standard rules of backgammon, including the doubling cube, gammons, hitting blots, bearing off, and all edge cases that make the game genuinely deep.

Roll the dice, move your checkers around the board toward your home quadrant, and bear them off before your opponent does the same. The deceptively simple objective hides extraordinary strategic depth — every dice roll presents decisions about blocking, attacking, and racing that separate good players from great ones.

Three AI difficulty levels give players of all experience a worthy opponent. Beginner AI plays randomly — ideal for learning the rules without pressure. Advanced AI evaluates positions with a scoring heuristic, playing defensively and going for hits opportunistically. Master AI makes smarter positional decisions and punishes tactical mistakes more reliably.

Like Sector Chess, Backgammon supports real-time online multiplayer through Firebase. Create a room, share the 6-character code with your opponent, and play instantly from anywhere in the world. The doubling cube adds a unique gambling mechanic — offer a double to pressure your opponent, or decline and concede one point rather than risk losing two.

Eight board themes — from Cyber and Walnut to Jade and Crimson — and four checker styles let you customize the visual experience. Flying checker animations make every move visually satisfying, and the Web Audio API synthesizes all sound effects in-browser.

🎯 How to Play

  • White moves clockwise (points 24→1), Black moves counter-clockwise (points 1→24).
  • Click Roll Dice to begin your turn — doubles give you four moves instead of two.
  • Click a point with your checkers to select, then click the destination point to move.
  • If a checker lands on a single opponent checker (a "blot"), it sends it to the bar.
  • Checkers on the bar must re-enter before any other move can be made.
  • Once all 15 checkers are in your home board, you can start bearing off.
  • The doubling cube lets you offer to double the stakes — opponent must accept or concede.

💡 Backgammon Tips & Strategy

  • Build points (two or more checkers on a point) to block your opponent — single checkers are vulnerable blots.
  • The bar is powerful: a checker on the bar must re-enter before any other move, potentially wasting an entire turn.
  • The prime (six consecutive blocked points) is the strongest defensive structure — it completely traps opponent checkers behind it.
  • Don't leave blots in your opponent's home board — they'll almost certainly get hit early in the game.
  • Use the doubling cube aggressively when you're clearly ahead — it forces your opponent into a difficult decision.
  • In a race (no contact), count your pip count versus opponent's — the player with fewer pips to move is winning.
  • Bear off efficiently: prioritize moving checkers from the highest points in your home board first.
  • Don't panic when hit early — re-entering from the bar can sometimes give you a tempo advantage.
